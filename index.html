 About Us

Rooted in the Soil,
Rising for the People

Registered under AP Societies Registration Act 35 of 2001 — Prakasam District, Andhra Pradesh

Mana Raithanna Foundation is a non-profit, non-commercial organisation headquartered in Edlurupadu Village, Zarugumalli Mandal. Founded on the belief that sustainable rural development begins at the grassroots, we work alongside farmers, women, youth and communities to build a self-reliant Andhra Pradesh.

From natural farming advocacy to women's empowerment, from sapling drives to disaster relief — our work spans the full spectrum of rural welfare, guided always by compassion, integrity, and respect for nature.

Empowerment

Sustainability

Community

Our mission is to foster holistic rural development by supporting farmers with sustainable agricultural practices, empowering women through skill-based training, providing quality education resources to children, and building community resilience through cultural preservation and social welfare initiatives.

We envision a prosperous Andhra Pradesh where every farmer thrives, every child learns, every woman leads, and every community stands united — in harmony with nature.

 Our Work

Programs & Initiatives

Comprehensive programs designed to uplift every segment of rural society — from field to family.

Agriculture & Environment

Sustainable farming & ecological restoration

Technical Farmer Support

Providing farmers with technical guidance, best practices, and problem-solving support to improve crop yields and reduce losses.

Organic Farming Training

Hands-on training in chemical-free, organic methods including soil enrichment techniques, composting and biological pest control.

Global Best Practices

Bringing internationally proven agricultural innovations and techniques to Andhra Pradesh's farming communities.

Subsidised Equipment

Facilitating access to quality agricultural tools and machinery at subsidised rates so every farmer can modernise affordably.

Natural Cow-Based Farming

Promoting Panchagavya and Jeevamruta — traditional cow-based biostimulants that revitalise soil health naturally.

Sapling Distribution & Eco Living

Sapling drives in villages, plastic-free living campaigns, and distribution of cloth and jute bags to reduce environmental harm.

Animal Welfare Stations

Setting up water troughs and food stations for stray animals along roadsides, ensuring their welfare year-round.

Empowerment & Education

Skills, learning & opportunity for women and youth

Women's Cottage Industry

Training women in tailoring, handicrafts, and cottage industry skills to generate sustainable income and self-reliance.

Free Youth Technical Training

Free technical and vocational training for youth alongside resume writing and interview preparation to boost employability.

School Supplies Distribution

Providing books, stationery, uniforms, and clothes to students from economically weaker backgrounds at the start of every academic year.

School Attendance Campaigns

Door-to-door awareness drives to encourage regular school attendance, especially for girls and first-generation learners.

Social Welfare & Community

Dignity, relief & cultural preservation for all

Orphan & Elder Support

Regular support and assistance for orphaned children and elderly individuals who lack family care or resources.

Seasonal Aid Drives

Buttermilk distribution in summer, blanket distribution in winter, and umbrella drives during monsoon for those in need.

Disaster Relief

Rapid response support during floods, cyclones, earthquakes and other natural calamities including food, clothing and shelter assistance.

Village Mini-Libraries

Establishing small reading rooms and libraries in villages to promote a culture of reading and lifelong learning.

Cultural Preservation

Organising meetings, events and festivals to document, celebrate and preserve the rich cultural heritage of Andhra Pradesh's rural communities.

Final Rites Assistance

Providing dignified funeral assistance to families who cannot afford proper last rites for their deceased loved ones.
